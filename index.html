Loan Risk Analytics Visualizations

Proof of concept for using visual analytics to find and convey insights through open source frameworks.

show more details

A series of three charts based on the same set of public home loan data, they illustrate how well designed visualizations can make complex financial instrument interactions accessible to a wider audience. By showing the same data from three different perspectives, an audience can form a richer understanding of its underlying patterns.

The 3D scatter plot is able to quickly provide a high level comparison of an entire bank's loan portfolio health and strategy.

The geospatial chart is able to show the regional variation of loan trends, as well as the dramatic change in delinquency rates that swept the US during the financial crisis. The network graph shows how loan type similarities converged over time, exposing loan portfolios to greater hidden risk.

design workshops 06

Visual Literacy Design Curriculum

Extensive data visualization training courses and workshops, developed and implemented across a Fortune 500 company.

 
show more details

The Visual Literacy Curriculum (VLC) is comprised of a large internal online community of practice, multiple online courses, and a hands on workshop. Centered around the themes of data representation, design principles, and storytelling, the curriculum was developed to improve a person's ability to understand, evaluate, and create effective data visualizations. Emphasizing an iterative, sketch driven design process, the VLC also helps trainees communicate in design terms, critique work, and create visualizations specifically tailored to their user's needs and data.

The VLC emphasizes the concept of a data team, comprised of seven primary roles, which may be filled with a flexible number of individuals of varying skill sets. Visualization is then used as the common medium of communication between data team members during projects.

During the intensive hands on VLC workshop, participants at all career levels are guided along an iterative data visualization development process, using numerous design techniques, as they work through case studies. Sketching, white boarding, and paper prototyping is stressed as the favored presentation medium -often with surprisingly effective results.
